 Title: Advances in Semantic Communication Technology

 

ABSTRACT: In recent years, semantic communication technology, which aims to achieve the effective transmission of semantic information, has become a focal point of academic research. Unlike traditional communication technologies that focus on error-free transmission at the bit-level symbol, semantic communication does not demand an exact match between the binary sequences received at the receiver and those sent from the transmitter. Instead, it requires that the semantic information recovered by the receiver aligns with the semantic content transmitted by the sender. By relaxing the stringent error requirements for information transmission, semantic communication technology is poised to overcome the transmission bottlenecks of current communication systems and is emerging as a competitive candidate for 6G technology.

 

BIO: Dr. Qi He received the B.S. and M.S. degrees in communication engineering from the University of Electronic Science and Technology of China (UESTC), Chengdu, China, in 2008 and 2011, respectively, where he is currently pursuing the Ph.D. degree in communication and information systems with the National Key Laboratory of Science and Technology on Communications. He was with Huawei Technologies Co., Ltd., from 2011 to 2013., His current research interests include wireless communications and networking, optimization theory, and artificial intelligence.

 

 

 


 

Dr. Yao Sun, University of Glasgow

Title: Semantic Communication in High-Data-Rate Wireless Networks

 

ABSTRACT: To cope with the ever-increasing request for higher data rates, it is foreseeable that available resources like spectrum or energy will gradually become scarce in traditional communication networks. Combined with the almost insurmountable Shannon limit, these destined bottlenecks are, therefore, motivating us to hunt for bold changes in the new design of future networks, i.e., making a paradigm shift from bit-based traditional communication to context-based semantic communication. This talk will first introduce the basis of semantic communications and its superiorities in future wireless communication systems. Then, several technical problems in semantic communication systems will be investigated. Last, some future research directions along with challenges should be discussed in this talk.

 

 

BIO: Dr. Yao Sun (Senior Member, IEEE) is currently a Lecturer with the James Watt School of Engineering, University of Glasgow, Glasgow, U.K. He has extensive research experience in the wireless communication area. His research interests include intelligent wireless networking, semantic communications, blockchain systems, and resource management in next-generation mobile networks. He has served as a TPC Member for several international flagship conferences, including ICC 2022, VTC Spring 2022, GLOBECOM 2020, WCNC 2019, and ICCT 2019. He has won the IEEE Communication Society of TAOS Best Paper Award in the 2019 ICC. He has served as the TPC Chair for UCET 2021. He has been the guest editor for special issues of several international journals.

 

 

Dr. Lei Luo, Chongqing University of Posts and Telecommunications

Title: Panoramic Video Coding Based on Spherical Distortion with Spatio-Temporal Dependency

 

ABSTRACT: Immersive communication is an important technical support for the realization of the metaverse, where users can watch 360-degree panoramic videos and interact with the virtual world. To ensure quality of service (QoS), it is necessary to transmit high-resolution panoramic video content, which causes panoramic video to consume a large amount of communication bandwidth. Therefore, improving the compression efficiency of panoramic video has become an urgent need. The panoramic video content seen by the viewer has the characteristics of spherical distribution, while the panoramic video is encoded using the ordinary planar video coding strategy, which makes the compression distortion cannot match the spherical distortion perceived by the viewer, thus reducing the efficiency of panoramic video coding. In addition, independent rate-distortion optimization of a single panoramic video frame can only achieve suboptimal coding efficiency, because it ignores the distortion propagation caused by inter-frame prediction of video coding. Therefore, we propose a spatio-temporal dependent spherical distortion model, and apply the model to the rate-distortion optimization and rate control of panoramic video coding, which significantly improves the efficiency of panoramic video coding.

 

BIO: Dr. Lei Luo (Member, IEEE) received the B.S. and Ph.D. degrees from Zhejiang University, Hangzhou, China, in 2008 and 2013, respectively. He is currently an Associate Professor at the School of Communications and Information Engineering, Chongqing University of Posts and Telecommunications, Chongqing, China. His research interests include video coding and transmission, 3-D video processing, and video analysis.

 

 

 

Dr. Jincheng Dai, Beijing University of Posts and Telecommunications

Title: Generative Intelligent Encoding Transmission

 

ABSTRACT: At present, generative Artificial Intelligence (GenAI) technology represented by large language models and diffusion models is a research hotspot in academia and industry. This report focuses on how generative AI can enable the innovation of coding and transmission technology in wireless communication. In this report, a new paradigm of generative coding and transmission with the characteristics of resilience and intelligence will be introduced, which can provide a stable and high-quality end-to-end service in a dynamic wireless channel environment. This report will demonstrate the multidimensional advantages and potential of this paradigm through a new perspective that leverages the received signal as a natural fine-grained condition to guide Bayesian generative modeling and build a new receiver architecture that enables future intelligent and simple end-to-end wireless transmission systems.

 

BIO: Dr. Jincheng Dai received a B.S. and Ph.D. degree from the Beijing University of Posts and Telecommunications (BUPT), Beijing, China, in 2014 and 2019. He is currently working with the Key Laboratory of Universal Wireless Communications, Ministry of Education, Beijing University of Posts and Telecommunications. His research focuses on semantic communications, source and channel coding, machine learning for communications.

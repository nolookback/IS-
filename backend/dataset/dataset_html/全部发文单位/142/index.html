[学术讲座]Far-Field Object Precise Point Positioning by Moving Transceivers
电子与信息工程学院　2024/12/13 17:20:00
  （钟沛基 2022280095）

嘉 宾 ：Prof. Dominic K. C. Ho,University of Missouri, USA

邀请人: 黄磊教授

时 间 :12月16日(周一) 09:30-10:30

地 点:   致信楼N710会议室

 

Abstract: A fundamental limitation of localization is that we are able to locate an object by direction of arrival only if it is in the far-field, instead of the precise point coordinates when it is in the near-field.  This talk introduces the concept of transceiver-aided localization, breaking through the barrier in locating a far-field object in coordinates rather than direction of arrival only.  The transceivers bridge between the sensors and the far-field object, which acts as a relay so that the probing signal can reach the object and return back to the sensors.  The minimum number of sensors and transceivers for localization is derived.  While we do not know the position and velocity of the transceivers, we show that we are able to perform joint estimation with the transceiver parameters to locate the object.  Simulation validates the theory and demonstrates the feasibility of the transceiver-based localization approach.

Biography:　

 Dr. Ho was born in Hong Kong.  He received the BSc degree with First Class Honors and the PhD degree in Electronic Engineering, both from the Chinese University of Hong Kong.  He was a research associate at the Royal Military College of Canada, a member of scientific staff at the Bell-Northern Research, and a faculty at the University of Saskatchewan, Canada.  Since 1997, he has been with the University of Missouri, where he is a professor in the Electrical Engineering and Computer Science Department.  His research interests are in sensor array processing, source localization, subsurface object detection, and wireless communications.  Dr. Ho has been active in the International Telecommunications Union (ITU) standard developments between 1995 and 2012.  He was the rapporteur of one recommendation and the editor of several others.  Dr. Ho was an Associate Editor of the IEEE Transactions of Signal Processing (2003-2006, 2009-2013) and the IEEE Signal Processing Letters (2004-2008).  He served as the Chair of the IEEE Sensor Array Multichannel (SAM) Technical Committee from 2013 to 2014 and the Past Chair in 2015.  He was Technical Co-Chair of the IEEE International Conference on Acoustics, Speech and Signal Processing (ICASSP) held in Shanghai, in 2016.  He is an inventor of 44 patents in the United States, Canada, Europe and Asia on geolocation and signal processing for wireless communications.  Dr. Ho is a fellow of the IEEE.

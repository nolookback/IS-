报告主题：Cell Cycle and Genome Instability

主讲嘉宾：Dr. Lin Deng

报告时间：2024年9月11日（星期三）10:00-12:00

报告地点：深圳大学丽湖校区守慧楼A6-606

Abstract

Cell cycle is tightly regulated to maintain normal cell function and proliferation. Cell cycle defects, resulted from endogenous or exogenous stresses, may lead to genome instability which is believed to be a driving force of tumorigenesis and cancer genome evolution. Genome instability and genome evolution is the key for cancer therapy and drug resistance. However, it remains elusive the form(s), the underlying mechanism and the consequences of genome instability. My group uses a combination of cutting-edge techniques such as in vitro biochemical reconstitution, genome engineering, high-throughput live cell imaging, high-resolution mass spectrometry and so forth, to address how cells coordinate growth and division under normal and pathological conditions. In combination with multiple models such as mammalian cell line, knockout mouse, and xenograft, we study the impact of cell cycle defects on genome stability. We have discovered some new phenomenon of genome instability and revealed the underlying molecular mechanisms. These findings greatly deepen our understanding of cell cycle and cancer genome evolution.

 

Speaker：

Dr. Lin Deng is a Principle Investigator (P.I.) at Shenzhen Bay Laboratory since 2020. He got his Ph.D. degree in Biochemistry from Dartmouth College in 2015, and received his postdoctoral training at Harvard Medical School from 2015 to 2020. His research interest is mainly focused on cell cycle regulation and genome instability. So far, he has published about 40 papers in journals like Molecular Cell, Current Biology, Nature, Nature Communications and so forth. He received honors including the 2014 Thomas-Copenhaver Fellow of Dartmouth College, 2015 E. Lucile Smith Award for Excellence in Biochemistry of Dartmouth Medical School, and 2020 Shenzhen High-Caliber Personnel.

Author introduction:

Zhengyu Zhang received the B.E. degree in electronic and information science and technology from Guangzhou University, Guangzhou, China, and the M.E. degree in electronics and communication engineering from Shenzhen University, Shenzhen, China, in 2018 and 2021, respectively. He is currently pursuing the Ph.D. degree with the National Institute of Applied Sciences, Rennes, France, and also with the Institute of Electronics and Digital Technologies Laboratory. His research interests include image/video quality assessment, visual perception, and deep learning.

 Abstract:

Light Field Image (LFI) has garnered remarkable interest and fascination due to its burgeoning significance in immersive applications. Although the abundant information in LFI enables a more immersive experience, it also poses a great challenge for the quality assessment of LFI, especially when the reference information is inaccessible. This work is dedicated to better evaluate the LFI quality in immersive multimedia. It can be divided into three parts. Firstly, we propose a No-Reference (NR) metric based on handcrafted features. This metric performs quality assessment by exploiting the spatio-angular textural variations of the distorted LFI. Then, we propose three NR metrics using deep-learning-based technologies. The principle of the first metric DeeBLiF is to divide LFI into multiple 2D patches and evaluate their quality via a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) model. The overall LFI quality is obtained by averaging the patch quality. The second metric PVBLiF, we improve DeeBLiF by utilizing 3D Pseudo Video Block Sequences (PVBSs) and performing pooling operation according to their saliency and variances. The third metric ASEM-BLiF designs a novel framework to effectively model the angular-spatial effect, which addresses the imbalance problem between angular and spatial information in LFI. In the last part, we will present our understanding and prospects for future researches on LFI.

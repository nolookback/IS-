讲座名称：Exploring hierarchical visual processes by integrating machine learning with neuroimaging

讲座时间：2024年11月12日下午3：00

讲座地点：深圳大学致理楼C座L3-1336

主讲人：葛一君


讲座简介：

The human visual system effortlessly interprets complex scenes, yet the neural mechanisms underlying this ability remain incompletely understood. This research investigates the intricate hierarchical processing of visual information within the brain, using a combination of neuroimaging techniques (7T fMRI, EEG) and machine learning. We explore three key aspects: 1) The role of the lateral prefrontal cortex (LPFC) in achieving visual uniformity, demonstrating its capacity to integrate disparate visual inputs and generalize to naturalistic movie scenes; 2) The interplay of feedforward and feedback mechanisms in sensory adaptation, focusing on the Flash Grab Effect and demonstrating that adaptation is driven by perceived, rather than retinal input; 3) The neural representation of 3D body pose, revealing a distributed cortical network with the posterior superior temporal sulcus (pSTS) encoding 3D view-independent pose information, showcasing the complex transformation of visual features from 2D to 3D. These findings advance our understanding of visual perception and awareness, highlighting the crucial roles of high-level brain regions and feedback mechanisms in constructing a coherent and adaptive visual experience.

葛一君，美国明尼苏达大学认知心理学博士，目前在日本理化学研究所脑科学研究中心担任特别研究员。先后以第一作者或通讯作者身份在Nature Communications、PNAS、Neuroscience等高水平期刊上发表多篇研究成果。

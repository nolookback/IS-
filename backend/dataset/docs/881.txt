讲座主要内容：
Using staggered
difference-in-differences (Staggered DID) methodology, we find that after the
P2P collapse, local stock investors become more risk
-
averse, which is manifested in a significant
reduction in their stock market trading activity. To further confirm that
investors become less likely to take risk, we find that the local collapse
makes investors less inclined to buy stocks of high-volatility and high-beta.
Investors in affected areas are also less willing to transfer funds to the
stock market, and less likely to trade unfamiliar stocks. In addition, to ruled
out other possible channels, we find that investors' trading performance does
not change significantly after the collapse, excluding the cognitive impairment
channel. There is no significant difference between investors' trading
behaviors on stocks with good and bad firm fundamentals, indicating that the
results are not caused by rational Adjustments.

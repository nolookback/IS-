Exploring hierarchical visual processes by
integrating machine learning with neuroimaging

讲座简介：
The human visual system effortlessly
interprets complex scenes, yet the neural mechanisms underlying this ability
remain incompletely understood. This research investigates the intricate
hierarchical processing of visual information within the brain, using a
combination of neuroimaging techniques (7T fMRI, EEG) and machine learning. We
explore three key aspects: 1) The role of the lateral prefrontal cortex (LPFC)
in achieving visual uniformity, demonstrating its capacity to integrate
disparate visual inputs and generalize to naturalistic movie scenes; 2) The
interplay of feedforward and feedback mechanisms in sensory adaptation,
focusing on the Flash Grab Effect and demonstrating that adaptation is driven
by perceived, rather than retinal input; 3) The neural representation of 3D
body pose, revealing a distributed cortical network with the posterior superior
temporal sulcus (pSTS) encoding 3D view-independent pose information,
showcasing the complex transformation of visual features from 2D to 3D. These
findings advance our understanding of visual perception and awareness,
highlighting the crucial roles of high-level brain regions and feedback
mechanisms in constructing a coherent and adaptive visual experience.

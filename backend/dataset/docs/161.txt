讲座摘要：
My
research explores the intricate landscape of consciousness in the brain, using
fMRI, anesthetics, and neuromodulation. This talk focuses on my recent work
investigating the dynamic interplay between the cerebral cortex and the
thalamus to unravel the neural basis of consciousness. I'll introduce the
concept of "functional geometry" and demonstrate how the organization
of the cortex, reflected in cortical gradients, is fundamentally linked to
conscious states. We'll then use these gradients as a compass to navigate the
thalamus, revealing how its structure relates to different kinds of information
processing. Anesthetics dramatically alter this brain map, offering valuable
clues about how consciousness emerges. By delving into specific cells and
neurochemicals within the thalamus, we can pinpoint the key elements affected
by anesthesia. Finally, I'll show how non-invasive brain stimulation targeting
the thalamus can directly influence conscious perception, opening exciting
avenues for understanding and potentially even manipulating consciousness
itself.

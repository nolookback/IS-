讲座摘要：
Neuroscience
made major progress in understanding the neural correlates of sensory, motor,
cognitive and affective functions. Despite these advances, the relationship of
brain and mind remains yet unclear, though. Specifically, the exact mechanisms
how neural activity transforms into mental activity as during consciousness,
self and their changes in mental disorders, are currently not known. Addressing
this gap in our knowledge, I present a novel approach, namely Spatiotemporal
Neuroscience, that allows integrating neural and mental features through their
shared spatial topographic and temporal dynamic features as their “common
currency” (Northoff et al. 2020). Such spatiotemporal approach is illustrated
by various lines of data and modelling on consciousness, self, and a mental
disorder, e.g., depression. I finally outline some core features of
Spatiotemporal Neuroscience concluding that it provides the promise of an
integrated brain mind model.

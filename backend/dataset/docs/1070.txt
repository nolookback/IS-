报告摘要：
This lecture has particular relevance to
data science (mathematics, computer science, and electrical engineering). It is
based on refereed research results that have been published very recently,
i.e., over the last 12 months. It is relevant for polynomial regression. An
accurate estimation of the polynomial degree is essential for better detection,
estimation, and prediction. This will demonstrate how the degree of a
polynomial can be estimated in a completely novel way and more successfully. The
starting point is uniformly sampled polynomial data, when the degree and the
coefficients of the polynomial are unknown. The first part deals with
noise-free data, while the second part deals with noisy data.

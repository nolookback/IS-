讲座主要内容：
We investigate the effect of
institutional investor attention and the public's uncertainty about asset value
on the financial market by studying a strategic trading problem, where the
strategic trader is subject to attention constraint and the asset value follows
a mean-reverting process. We show that the limited institutional investor attention
endogenously generates a private signal for the strategic trader, precluding a
fully revealed price. In contrast, the transparency of the asset value improves
the market efficiency. Furthermore, the trading aggressiveness, expected
payoff, and informativeness of price increase with institutional investor
attention, while market liquidity decreases with this attention. On the other
hand, the effect of the public's uncertainty about asset value is uncertain and
depends on the remainder of the strategic trader's information advantage. This
feature explains different empirical findings pertaining to the relationship
between public news and market liquidity.

讲座摘要：
Writing systems vary in how readers access
word meanings from their written forms. Phonology plays a crucial role in visual
word recognition in alphabetic languages. In contrast, in logographic scripts such
as Chinese, readers can access word meanings before activating their phonological
representations. This has been shown not only in individual word recognition
but also in word processing during reading sentences. When reading sentences,
individuals process the word they are currently focused on, as well as words
nearby in their peripheral vision. Researchers are interested in the time
course of processing different types of linguistic properties of words in both
fovea and parafovea. In this presentation, I will introduce several eye-tracking
studies examining how different phonological units of Chinese characters are
processed during Chinese sentence reading. Our results challenge the idea that
phonology universally mediates lexical access.

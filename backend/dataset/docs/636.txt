讲座摘要：
Prediction, a fundamental process
that links past, present and future, mediates many cognitive functions that are
crucial for survival. In speech, especially when we are speaking, prediction is
the key process for online optimizing the outcome of the action. In this talk,
I will present recent studies from our lab to illustrate the neural mechanisms
of prediction in speech production and control at multiple levels of the
computational purposes, potential algorithms, functional hierarchy and neural
pathways. Via the research window of speech, a canonical neural computation of
prediction is proposed and inspires future investigation in cognitive
neuroscience.

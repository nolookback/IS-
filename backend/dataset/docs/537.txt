讲座摘要：
Escape is a crucial aspect of defensive
behavior, and it relies on two distinct circuits: the reactive fear circuity
and the cognitive fear circuity. However, the precise contributions of these
circuits to specific stages of human escaping remain poorly understood. In this
study, we conducted intracranial electroencephalography (iEEG) recordings from
epilepsy patients while they performed a modified flight initiation distance
task.
We found that the high gamma
activity of the brain regions in fear circuity encoded the threatening information.
On the other hand, the reactive fear circuits exhibited higher high gamma
activity during the actual escaping stage, particularly in response to rapid
attacks. Furthermore, we observed that the amygdala suppresses the cognitive
fear circuit under rapid attacks.
These
novel insights highlight the collaborative involvement of both reactive and
cognitive fear circuits in orchestrating the entire human escaping
process.
By elucidating the interplay
between these circuits, our study provides valuable contributions to our
understanding of the neural mechanisms underlying defensive behavior in humans.

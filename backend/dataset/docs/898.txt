讲座主要内容：
Using
data from a Twitter-like investor social trading platform, we document evidence
consistent with the self-enhancing transmission bias. Investors are more likely
to broadcast their better-performing stocks. Their followers are more likely to
buy the broadcasted stocks than the non-broadcasted stocks, and the strength of
the follow-up purchase is related to the perceived credibility of the
broadcasts. Both the strength of the selected broadcasting and the strength of
the follow-up purchase vary with stock characteristics in a way that
facilitates the spread of
high-variance,
high-skewness, and extrapolative strategies. We also document that the social
network features influential, disproportionately important nodes.

讲座简介：
Working
memory, the ability to temporarily maintain and manipulate information to serve
flexible behavioral goals, is central to human intelligence and fundamental to
the field of cognitive neuroscience. While it has been proposed that
(long-term) memories are not mere reflections of past perceptual experiences
but rather re-representations of the past, whether and how abstraction occurs
in working memory remains largely unexplored. In this talk, I will present our
recent work investigating the neural mechanisms underlying abstraction
processes in human working memory. Using functional magnetic resonance imaging
(fMRI) and electroencephalography (EEG) in combination with behavioral
paradigms requiring manipulation of working memory contents, I will demonstrate
the neural coding principles for abstract task information and for structural
generalization in working memory across distributed cortical regions, and
discuss how abstraction in working memory contributes to the execution of
higher cognitive functions.

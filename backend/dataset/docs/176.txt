讲座简介：
Humans
possess a remarkable ability to communicate with others, inferring what is
meant from what is and is not being said. Despite recent progress in
understanding the neural bases of this important class of social function,
little is known about when and how humans acquire this ability, whether basic
neurocognitive functions such as executive control and social reasoning
interact with one another in support of communicative development, and how
individual differences in autism spectrum traits influence communicative
reasoning across development?
In this talk,
I will show a series of studies that use model-based fMRI with cross-sectional
and longitudinal data to elucidate the neurobehavioral changes in communicative
reasoning during the transition in and out of adolescence
––
a critical period for cognitive and social development.

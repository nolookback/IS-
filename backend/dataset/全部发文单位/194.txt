讲座简介：
A
hallmark of human intelligence is goal-directed behavior. In other words, for
the same sensory input, our brain can process it flexibly and generate
different responses that suit different goals. This flexibility is supported by
a set of cognitive functions termed cognitive control. While much progress has
been made towards the understanding of how cognitive control guides other
cognitive functions to achieve goal-directed behavior, the origins of the
control signals are understudied. Recent advances in behavioral paradigms and
neuroimaging methods allow us to investigate this question through the lens of
task representations, which encode key information about the goal, input,
output and their transformations. Here, I will present two studies to show that
task representations are rich, such that they may contain other information
that signals the demand of cognitive control and benefits task performance.

Abstract:
Plant diseases result in an annual economic loss exceeding $220 billion worldwide. Gram-negative plant bacterial pathogens reply on type III secretion system and type III effectors to cause disease. Various type III effectors, like HopU1 and AvrPtoB, function as enzymes to undermine plant innate immunity. Through research using the model plant
Arabidopsis thaliana
, scientists have identified key players molecular interactions between plants and pathogens.  NPR1, NPR3, and NPR4 have been identified as receptors for the plant defense hormone salicylic acid. Additionally, EDS1 serves as a central hub in pathogen-associated molecular pattern (PAMP)-triggered immunity, effector-triggered immunity, plant basal defense, salicylic acid-mediated plant immunity, and systemic acquired resistance. Despite extensive research over many years, the precise molecular and biochemical functions of EDS1 remained unclear. Our findings indicate that EDS1 acts as a transcriptional co-activator with acid activation domains and interacts with transcription factors to activate the expression of plant defense genes.

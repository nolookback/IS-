讲座介绍：
Prediction is a fundamental process that
shapes many cognitive functions. Recently, studies have demonstrated that the
motor system may be one of the sources that generate prediction. In this talk,
I will first present some recent studies from our lab to illustrate the multifaceted
functions of motor-based prediction in the context of speech production and
control in normal populations, as well as the selective deficits that may lead
to auditory hallucinations in schizophrenia patients. Next, to further zoom out
by taking the functional similarity between the motor-based prediction and
attention, I will present some preliminary results suggesting the possibility
of a relation between the motor and the attentional system. Via the
investigation of motor-based prediction, a motor-orientated framework emerges
and inspires us to ponder the relation between action and cognition.
主讲人介绍：
田兴，北京大学物理学学士，美国马里兰大学神经与认知科学博士。现任上海纽约大学神经与认知科学研究员，博导。田兴教授采用电生理学（脑磁图、脑电图、颅内脑电）和神经影像学（功能磁共振成像）等技术手段结合行为测量和计算建模，研究言语和语言、运动感觉整合、以及记忆、想象等人类高级认知功能。主要工作包括创新性的结合想象和语言产生探索运动感觉系统交互的神经机制。已在认知神经科学领域的专业杂志上发表

讲座简介：
Pain perception varies
widely across individuals. The same pain stimulus may be intolerably painful
for someone, yet barely perceivable by another. What neural activity encodes
such dramatic individual differences in pain perception? In this talk, I
classify individual differences in pain into two broad categories: pain
sensitivity and pain discriminability, corresponding to the absolute and
difference thresholds in psychophysics. I will then present two recent studies
that developed replicable and generalizable neural indicators of these pain
individual differences using large scale datasets. Altogether, these studies
advance the understanding of pain mechanisms and facilitating individualized
pain treatment.
